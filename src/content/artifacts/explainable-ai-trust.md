## Explanatory Document

This visual artifact explains how Explainable AI (XAI) supports trust in large language models such as GPT, Claude, Gemini, and LLaMA. These models are powerful but difficult to interpret because their outputs are produced through complex, distributed interactions across many parameters. As a result, users may not always understand why a model generated a specific response. The infographic highlights key challenges, including black-box complexity, unreliable post-hoc explanations, bias in training data, and regulatory concerns in high-stakes fields such as healthcare, finance, and law.

The design also connects explainability with validation techniques and performance metrics. While XAI helps users understand model behavior, validation and metrics provide measurable evidence that a model is reliable, fair, and robust. Techniques such as accuracy testing, F1 score, confusion matrices, bias testing, robustness testing, and human evaluation help identify weaknesses and improve accountability. The artifact uses a clean, section-based layout to make the topic accessible to a non-technical audience while still demonstrating important AI/ML concepts for a professional portfolio.
